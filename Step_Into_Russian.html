The Neuroscience

In 1997, a Cornell University fMRI study changed everything we know about language in the adult brain.

Researchers discovered that second languages learned in adulthood occupy a separate, dedicated region of Broca's area — distinct from the native language. This region can become just as automatic, just as effortless, and just as truly yours as your first language. The Russian Method is built entirely around activating it.
