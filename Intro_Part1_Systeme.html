The Russian Method — Part 1

Welcome to Your Russian Journey

This is not a course where you memorize grammar rules and vocabulary lists. This is an immersion experience — designed to build a separate language center that controls Russian in your brain, the same way you built your native language as a child. Not by memorizing. By absorbing.

What Part 1 will do for you

Part 1 consists of 10 lessons. Each lesson has a short text, an audio recording, and a practice assignment with four blocks of exercises. Together they develop all four language skills: listening, speaking, reading and writing.

  • Train your ear to recognize and process Russian sounds naturally
  • Build your first blocks of automatic vocabulary — words you feel, not translate
  • Begin developing real fluency through rhythm, repetition and immersion
  • Connect you with a community of learners on the same journey

Three principles to keep in mind

1. Don't translate — visualize.

When you hear or read Russian, create mental images. Let your brain build direct connections between the Russian word and the reality it represents — with no English in between.

2. Repetition is not failure — it's the method.

You will repeat the same text many times. This is not because you are slow. This is because repetition is how language enters the brain permanently.

3. Progress is not always visible — but it is always happening.

Some days will feel easy. Some days will feel like nothing is sticking. Trust the process. Your brain is working even when it doesn't feel like it.

The Pre-Lesson Ritual

Before each lesson you will watch a short video. This is not a warm-up exercise — it is a conscious mental switch.

Here is what is actually happening: your brain has spent years operating in English. Every time you sit down to learn Russian, your English language center is already active — filtering, translating, analyzing. The ritual interrupts that pattern. It signals to your brain that a different mode is beginning.

By choosing a Russian name and speaking those words out loud, you are doing something precise and intentional: you are temporarily stepping out of your English identity and into the role of a Russian speaker. A child. Someone who absorbs without judgment, without translation, without resistance.

This is not a metaphor. It is a practical tool — and it works because your brain responds to the context you give it. Give it Russian context, and it will begin to build a Russian response.

  • Choose your Russian name — Oleg, Maria, or any other Russian name you like.
  • Say the following out loud before every lesson:

Я не говорю сейчас по-английски.

Моё имя Олег. / Моё имя Мария.

Я — ребёнок.

Однако, я быстро расту.

Я учу русский язык.

Do it every time. Without exception.

A Note on Dictionaries

If you encounter a word you don't understand, here is exactly what to do:

  1. Look the word up and read the translation or explanation.
  2. Do not say the translation aloud in your native language — not even quietly.
  3. Instead, focus on the images and associations that arise as you read the explanation. Let the meaning form as a picture, not as an English word.
  4. Go back to the Russian sentence containing that word and repeat it — again and again — until the unfamiliar word begins to feel connected to your own associations.

This is how unknown words stop being foreign. They don't get translated — they get absorbed.
