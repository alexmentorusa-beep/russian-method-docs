The Russian Method — Lesson [N]

Practice Assignment — [Topic]

Work through each block in order — each one builds on the previous. Take your time, be patient with yourself, and trust the process. The goal is not perfection — it's consistent repetition.

Block 1

Listen & Repeat

This block trains your ear and begins building your Russian sound system. You are not memorizing — you are absorbing.

  1. Read the text in English mode — visualize everything you read as vivid mental images. Do not pronounce anything aloud.
  2. Switch to Russian mode. Start the audio. Follow the Russian text with your eyes and repeat simultaneously with the speaker — don't worry about your pronunciation, just follow.
  3. Keep repeating with the speaker until it starts to feel easy and natural.
  4. Close your eyes. With the audio still playing, repeat 5–6 more times.
🎥

Your assignment: Record a short video of your best, most confident repetition and share it in the Telegram group.

Block 2

Write & Build

This block activates your memory and begins turning passive vocabulary into something you own.

  1. Start the audio and repeat with the speaker as in Block 1 — without stopping the recording, write down every Russian word that comes to mind as you listen.
  2. Repeat this drill 5–6 times, writing down new words each time.
  3. Use your collected words to build 2–3 short sentences in Russian. They don't have to be perfect.
  4. Read your sentences aloud.
💬

Your assignment: Share in the Telegram group — what came naturally, what felt difficult, and where you got stuck.

Block 3

Speed Up

This block builds fluency and automatism. When words flow fast, they stop being translated — they become instinct.

  1. Take the sentences you built in Block 2.
  2. Say the first sentence slowly and clearly. Then again — a little faster. Then faster again.
  3. Keep going until it sounds like one flowing word.
  4. Repeat with each of your sentences.
🎙️

Your assignment: Record yourself during this exercise and share the audio in the Telegram group.

Block 4

Tell Your Story

This block is where everything comes together. You are no longer repeating — you are speaking.

  1. Write a short story (3–5 sentences) using words and phrases from the lesson. It can be simple — even silly. Just make it yours.
  2. Read your story aloud several times until it flows.
  3. Share how the lesson felt — what surprised you, what clicked, what still feels foggy.
📝

Your assignment: Post your story and your impressions in the Telegram group.
