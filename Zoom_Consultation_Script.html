Шаг 7 · 20–24 мин
🧠 Метод погружения
Студент уже заинтересован — коротко, ярко, убедительно
From everything you've just told me, I think this would work really well for you. So let me tell you about my method.
Традиционный подход — и почему он не работает
Most people learn a foreign language the same way. They go to class, memorize word lists, drill grammar rules, do exercises — and after months or even years of that, they still freeze up the moment someone actually speaks to them in real life.
That happens because the traditional approach treats language like a subject — like math or history. It builds a system of rules and translations in your head. So every time you hear a Russian word, your brain first finds the English equivalent, checks the grammar rule, constructs the sentence — and by the time all that is done, the moment has passed. It feels slow, unnatural, exhausting.
Мой подход — как это работает на самом деле
My method works completely differently. We don't study Russian — we acquire it. There's no translation, no grammar tables, no memorization. Instead, we build a separate Russian language center directly in your brain — one that operates independently, without going through English at all.
The core technique is simultaneous repetition with visualization. You hear a phrase in Russian, you see it, and at the same moment you picture the meaning as a real image or scene in your mind — not a word in English. You repeat it out loud almost simultaneously with the recording. Your brain starts connecting sound directly to meaning, bypassing translation entirely.
Over time, Russian stops feeling like a foreign language you're decoding — and starts feeling like a second mode your brain naturally switches into. You think in Russian. You respond in Russian. Without effort.
Почему это работает для взрослых
A lot of people believe adults can't learn languages the way children do. That's not quite true. Children acquire language naturally because they have no choice — they're immersed, they can't translate, they just absorb. Adults can do the same thing, but we need a structured method that deliberately bypasses the translation habit we've already built. That's exactly what this method does.
Most of my students reach a confident conversational level in six months to a year. Not because they work harder, but because they work in the right direction.
Моя история
I know this method works — because I've lived it. When I moved to the United States, I had zero English. None. And yet within six months, I reached an advanced level — not by memorizing vocabulary lists or grinding through grammar books, but by using the exact same immersive approach I now teach. That experience is what inspired me to help other adults do the same thing with Russian.
Does that make sense — the difference between memorizing and actually acquiring a language?
There's actually a study published in Nature in 1997 that used brain imaging to show exactly this. Adults who learn a second language build a completely separate speech center in the brain — distinct from their native language. Once properly developed, it works just as automatically. That's what we're building together.
What you described with [Duolingo / the grammar course] is a perfect example of the memory approach. You learned, then you forgot. That's not your fault — that's exactly how that method works. It's designed for memory, not for speech.
